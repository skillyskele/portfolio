Analog Radio Receiver

Exploring analog design principles through the creation of a functional AM radio receiver.

Project Overview

This project involved designing and building a basic AM radio receiver to deepen my understanding of analog circuits. The receiver consists of an RLC tuning circuit, an RF amplifier, an AM demodulator, and an audio amplifier. The goal was to design a circuit capable of receiving and processing AM signals from a nearby station.

Stages of the Radio Receiver

  • Antenna + RLC Circuit: Captures and tunes the signal to the desired frequency.
  • RF Amplifier: Boosts the weak signal for further processing.
  • AM Demodulator: Extracts the audio signal from the modulated carrier wave.
  • Audio Amplifier: Increases the audio signal strength to drive the speaker.

Circuit Design

Each stage was designed using standard analog components:

  • Tuning Circuit: A variable capacitor and inductor form an RLC circuit tuned to 1 MHz for AM reception.
  • RF Amplifier: A common-emitter amplifier for signal gain.
  • AM Demodulator: A diode and RC filter to rectify and demodulate the signal.
  • Audio Amplifier: A Class A amplifier for clear audio output.

Results and Testing

The radio receiver successfully tuned into a local AM station. Challenges included fine-tuning the RLC circuit and minimizing interference from nearby frequencies.
